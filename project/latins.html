LATIN ARTISTS

SEBASTIÁN YATRA

HOMETOWN: Medellín, Colombia
BORN: October 15, 1994 - GENRE: Pop Latino

If there's anything that is necessary for a singer of romantic ballads, it's genuine thoughtfulness—and Sebastián Yatra has that in spades. Born Sebastián Obando Giraldo in 1994 in Medellín, Colombia, Yatra moved to the U.S. at age five and grew up in what may be the world's most creatively fertile city for Latin pop: Miami. Yatra began singing and writing his own songs when he was 12, taking classes to improve his skills on guitar, piano, and voice. He had his first hit at age 19 with 2013's “El Psicólogo,” a charming acoustic ballad that detailed hidden love and psychological complexity. It performed well in Colombia and Latin America, but to really ascend to Latin pop's heights, the singer had to embrace the burgeoning pop-reggaetón scene. In 2017, he nailed it with the reggaetón- and EDM-brushed “Traicionera,” which retained the powerful sweetness of his vocal delivery but gave fans the tropical beats they craved. Partly on the strength of that single, his 2018 debut album, Mantra, topped charts in Colombia and the Latin Pop Albums chart in the U.S. Collaborators came knocking, including Colombian superstar Carlos Vives, who brought Yatra onto the vallenato-tinged “Robarte un Beso.” In 2019, Yatra followed up his successful first album with Fantasía, which spawned many singles and established him as an ongoing force in Latin pop.

PISO 21

GENRE: Pop Latino

Mixing sleek hi-hat patterns with graceful guitar melodies, Piso 21, along with fellow Medellín artists KAROL G, Maluma, and J Balvin, added an urban edge to Colombian pop. The foursome was founded in 2007 when Pablo “Pablito” Mejía, Juan David “El Profe” Huertas, David “Dim” Escobar, and David “Lorduy” Hernández met at a friend's party. Their band name was inspired by the fact that the festive gathering was held at a 21st-floor apartment. Piso 21 took their time before releasing their well-received eponymous full-length debut, a soothing, sun-kissed, guitar-laden album, in 2012. They drew even more widespread recognition after pivoting toward a more street-style sound with 2014's “Suele Suceder,” featuring Puerto Rican singer and reggaetón royalty Nicky Jam. The sonic shift couldn't have been timed better as it put the band in the forefront of the reggaetón explosion that within three years would see the success of Luis Fonsi & Daddy Yankee's worldwide hit “Despacito.” That year, the troupe signed with Warner Music, dropped “Me Llamas,” the theme song for Colombian telenovela series La ley del corazón, and demonstrated their sensual trap bravado in their sophomore album, 2018's Ubuntu, by introducing atmospheric synths to their club-ready sound. Where Piso 21 will go next musically is impossible to predict but exciting to imagine.

CAMILO

HOMETOWN: Medellín, Colombia
BORN: March 16, 1994 - GENRE: Pop Latino

A member of the group Los Ninos Rebeldes, singer-songwriter Camilo won the Colombian youth talent competition El Factor Xs in 2007.
∙ “Desconocidos,” his 2008 breakthrough single, featured his future brothers-in-law Mau y Ricky, as well as Manuel Turizo.
∙ Camilo enjoyed a second career in television, making appearances on Colombian telenovelas and the children's program Bichos before returning to music full time in 2014.
∙ A prolific songwriter, he's cowritten tracks for Juanes (“Pa Dentro”), Bad Bunny (“Si Estuviésemos Juntos”), and Bomba Estéro (“Ayo”).
∙ Shakira guested on a remix of his 2019 quintuple-Platinum hit, “Tutu,” which also featured Pedro Capó and won Best Pop Song at the 2020 Latin Grammys.
∙ His 2020 LP, Por Primera Vez, topped Billboard's Latin Pop Albums chart and was nominated for Best Latin Pop or Urban Album at the 2021 Grammy Awards.

MAU AND RICKY

ORIGIN: Caracas, Venezuela - GENRE: Pop Latino

Venezuela-born brothers Mau y Rick's hits mix Latin pop and reggaetón, and they've also written popular songs for some of Latin music's biggest modern stars.
∙ The sons of Latin-pop icon Ricardo Montaner, Mau y Ricky began playing music as children and first worked professionally in their father's backing band.
∙ The pair served as battle advisers alongside their dad, who was a coach on the 2012 and 2013 seasons of The Voice Colombia.
∙ Mau y Ricky's 2016 single “Voy Que Quemo” was a streaming hit that also received radio airplay throughout Latin America.
∙ Their many successful songwriting credits include Ricky Martin's 2016 smash “Vente Pa' Ca” and Becky G. & Natti Natasha's 2019 hit “Sin Pijama.”
∙ On the strength of their 2017 debut EP, Arte, the duo received a Latin Grammy nomination for Best New Artist.
∙ Their 2019 debut studio LP, Para Aventuras y Curiosidades, made the Billboard Latin Pop Top 10 and featured “Desconocidos,” their hit with Colombian artists Manuel Turizo and Camilo.

CHRISTIAN NODAL

HOMETOWN: Caborca, Sonora, México
BORN: MJanuary 11, 1999 - GENRE: Música Mexicana

Christian Nodal has mixed feelings about the modern world. On the one hand, he probably wouldn't have his career without it—a fact to which his millions of heart-emoji-posting social-media followers can attest. On the other, his is an art forged deeply in the past, a gentle pushback against contemporary sounds in favor of older styles of Mexican music combined in novel but reverential ways. His fans see him as the great hope of regional Mexican, an artist keeping the flame of tradition alive for listeners on both sides of the border. To Nodal, he's just an old man in a young man's body.
Born in 1999 in the Sonoran city of Caborca, bordering Arizona, he started playing music as a child and was writing his own songs by middle school. His highlights—“Aquí Abajo,” “No Te Contaron Mal,” “Adiós Amor,” “Te Fallé”—channel the romanticism of classic Mexican singers like Vicente Fernández and Juan Gabriel, but also reflect a clever blend of styles: mariachi, norteño, cumbia, even country. He's not trying to cross over. But as demographics change, Nodal has gained a foothold with Latinx communities everywhere, not by assimilating his sound, but by keeping it pure.
